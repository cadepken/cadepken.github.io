About Me

I'm a third year electrical engineering student with a passion for ocean engineering. I'm interested in developing underwater communication and navigation systems, ocean instrumentation, and UAV/ROVs.

Recent Work

Georgia Tech Low Frequency Lab

A line receiver box I helped build conducting research at Georgia Tech over a 10 week period during summer '24. My research focused on processing very low frequency (VLF) radio signals sent during the 2024 total solar eclipse using MATLAB.

Working in the NCSU Libraries Makerspace

I've worked as a makerspace student advisor since my freshman year, and along with that comes a lot of problem solving and machine repair, especially when the worst happens to a patron's project -- like a nasty 3-D print failure.

IEEE-HKN Beta Eta Fall '23 Inductions

I'm both a member of IEEE-HKN and an officer, serving as the recording secretary for the 2024-2025 school year. Between HKN and volunteering as an ECE Ambassador, I'm able to make a personal difference in STEM outreach initiatives.

Study Abroad in Florence, Italy, Summer '23

I studied abroad in summer 2023 for my history minor. As a part of the program, we picked one art piece to see in Florence. I chose "Harbour with Lighthouse and Ships" by Salvator Rosa. I took a naval history class my freshman year, and I've been hooked on tall ships every since.

Bugfest and Scientific Communication

I spent a year as a staff writer for the Technician , NC State's student run newspaper. I covered a dozen or so events, but one of my favorites was Bugfest, as it was one of my first exposures to science communication (and bug-infused foods).

HESO 257: Backpacking & HESO 258: Rock Climbing

As a part of building my outdoor skills, I've taken two outdoor classes during my time at NCSU.

First Year Engineering Design Day (FEDD)

During my freshman year, my group placed 3rd in the arcade game category, which challenged students to design and build their own arcade game.

Find Tuffy Finalist

Find Tuffy was a campus-wide scavenger hunt my freshman year. I found the hidden tuffy figure 18 times over the semester to place second in the campus competition.
